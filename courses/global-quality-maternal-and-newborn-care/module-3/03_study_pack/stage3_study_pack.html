2. Active Recall Flashcard Matrix (Anki-Ready)

Core Concept / Term Definition Operational Relevance
Implementation Gap The recognized disparity between knowing what effective interventions exist and effectively applying them on a global scale. Contributes to persistent high maternal and newborn mortality/morbidity despite increased access to healthcare facilities for childbirth.
Respectful Maternity Care (RMC) A fundamental human right for childbearing women, encompassing seven universal rights including freedom from harm, right to information/consent, privacy, dignity, equality, timely care, and liberty/autonomy. Aims to uphold women's human rights during childbirth, addressing widespread disrespect and abuse that undermine these rights.
Obstetric Violence A form of gender-based violence experienced during childbirth within institutional medical settings, including physical, psychological, and emotional harm, lack of informed consent, coercive procedures, and denial of autonomy. Prevalent issue (e.g., 41.6% of women experienced abuse in LMICs), particularly affecting younger and less-educated women, undermining quality of care and human rights.
Culturally Appropriate Care Skilled maternity care that respects women's beliefs, values, and choices. Associated with increased access to care, improved satisfaction, and better health outcomes for mothers and newborns; requires provider cultural competencies and supportive health system policies.
Community-Based Maternal and Newborn Care (CMNC) Interventions such as Community Health Worker (CHW) home visits and peer support groups. Can improve demand for and access to maternal and newborn health services, influence health-seeking behaviors, promote facility births, and are often cost-effective in LMICs.

3. Clinical Discussion Prompts

Discussion Track 1: Balancing Clinical Effectiveness and Human Rights
Given the significant gaps in adherence to evidence-based clinical guidelines (e.g., AMTSL, magnesium sulfate for pre-eclampsia) alongside the widespread prevalence of mistreatment and obstetric violence, how should healthcare systems prioritize and integrate efforts to improve both clinical outcomes and respectful maternity care simultaneously in resource-constrained settings?

Discussion Track 2: Multi-Component Strategies for Quality Improvement
The text highlights that multi-component interventions are most successful, integrating both facility-based (e.g., audit, training) and community-based (e.g., women's groups, CHWs) strategies. Discuss the potential challenges and synergistic benefits of implementing such comprehensive approaches, particularly in bridging the "implementation gap" in low- and middle-income countries.

Discussion Track 3: Addressing Systemic Barriers to Workforce Performance
The text identifies several barriers to effective midwifery performance, including inadequate staffing, long working hours, poor remuneration, and insufficient access to training, alongside the need for essential resources and infrastructure. How can health systems effectively address these systemic issues to ensure a competent, motivated, and supported workforce capable of delivering high-quality, respectful maternal and newborn care?
